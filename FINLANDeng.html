Finland



Normal education lasts for 9 years and it is divided into two key stages: pre-primary school and basic education. However, after completing basic education, students are given a unique opportunity to choose their path. They have two great alternatives: Matriculation examination or Vocational qualifications.
The lessons are 45 minutes. And what is pleasing is that education in schools is free, this makes it accessible to everyone.
Although there are no organized clubs in schools, in every city you can find "youth houses, which provide teenagers with unique hobbies.
The evaluation system is a 10-point. As for learning second languages, German and French are the most popular. Also starting from the 7th grade, obligatory study of Swedish begins.
I would especially like to note that there are no requirements for school uniforms at school. Lessons give children the freedom to choose a place to study, whether it's desks, armchairs or the floor, and in good weather even fresh air becomes a learning platform. This creates an education system where diversity and freedom of choice are the basis of school life.
